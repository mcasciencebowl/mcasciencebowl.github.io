About MCASB

The Middlesex County Academy Science Bowl is a science-based competition hosted for students in grades 5-8. In contrast to other online competitions, this competition makes participants directly compete with other participants with a buzzer system, following a similar format to quiz bowl.

Location

Middlesex County Academy, 100 Technology Drive, Edison, NJ 08837

Type

Middle school competition, Teams of 3-5 people

Fees

$10 per person

Competition Format

How does MCASB work?

Preliminary Round

In the preliminary round, participants take a test along with their team members in order to qualify for the playoff rounds. The top teams on this test will move on to the playoffs. The preliminary round team test will consist of multiple-choice and short answer questions.

Playoff Rounds

Teams that qualify for these rounds will be put into a double-elimination bracket based on their scores from preliminary rounds. The teams who make it to playoffs will be eligible for team prizes as well as individual top scorer prizes.

The playoffs will be run in quiz bowl format. We will explain this format during our opening ceremony, although you can check our FAQ section for information about the quiz bowl format in advance.

Consolation Rounds

Teams that don't qualify for the playoff round will be put into the consolation rounds. Teams in the consolation round will not be eligible for a top team prize. However, individuals on teams competing in the consolation round will be eligible for an individual prize.

These will also be run in a quiz bowl format. We will explain this format during our opening ceremony, or you can read up about it in the FAQs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Before the Competition

Why is this competition being held?

The Middlesex County Academy is a school dedicated to STEM education and awareness. Our passion for science is what drove us to initiate this competition to help young students discover their passion and learn more in the sciences.

Is this competition for me?

This competition is for anyone who shares a passion for science or simply wants to try their hands on it. It is, however, restricted to students in middle school (grades 5 - 8).

How should I prepare for MCASB? What do I get tested on?

MCASB competition tests concepts in physics, chemistry, biology, and environmental sciences at the middle school level. The goal of this competition is to encourage learning in the sciences. To prepare optimally, we encourage that you improve upon your conceptual understanding instead of memorizing facts. Remember, there is a test section and a quiz bowl section, so you may want to learn in advance how the quiz bowl format works. Otherwise, we can explain how quiz bowl works in our opening ceremony.

Are there any prizes for best-performing teams?

Indeed! The top 3 competing teams who make it to playoffs will receive prizes and swag from us. In addition, the top 2 individuals in the playoffs and the top 2 individuals in the consolation rounds will get prizes.



During the Competition

How does the Quiz Bowl Format Work?

2 teams will be pitted against each other. Each member on the team will have a buzzer (we will be using an online service for this). The moderator will begin to read "clues" about a scientific term, location, scientist, or concept. Any competitor on either team can buzz in while the moderator is still speaking and answer. However, please note that only the buzzer can answer the question.

If the buzzer answers correctly within the first couple of seconds, their team will get 15 points. If the buzzer answers incorrectly within the first couple of seconds, their team will lose 5 points.

If the buzzer answers correctly before the time is up, their team will get 10 points. If the buzzer answers incorrectly or nobody buzzes before the time is up, their team will not lose any points.

If a team gets a question right, they will be entitled to 3 bonus questions. These bonuses can only be answered by the team who got the initial question right, and teams will be able to collaborate (they can speak to each other) to provide an answer to each bonus within 10 seconds of the moderator reading the question. If they get a bonus question correct, they can get 10 extra points. They will have a shot at each of the 3 bonuses.

Once the bonus rounds are over (if they even happen), the teams will move on to the next question.

What am I supposed to do on the day of the competition?

On the day of the competition, you first attend our opening ceremony. All competition logistics and rules will be explained there. We will be sending out room assignments regarding where your team needs to go for each round. We'll be making regular announcements with any changes.

I don't know what to do during the competition. What should I do?
